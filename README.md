# retail-sales-eda
Exploratory Data Analysis on retail sales performance across online, offline, and hybrid channels

Retail Sales EDA — Online, Offline & Hybrid Channels
📌 Project Overview
An exploratory data analysis (EDA) project examining sales performance across three retail channel types: online, offline, and hybrid. The goal was to uncover patterns and relationships between key variables including sales volume, discount rates, promotion duration, and initial product pricing.
🛠️ Tools & Libraries

Python
Pandas & NumPy — data manipulation
Matplotlib & Seaborn — data visualization

📊 Key Findings
1. Sales Volume Distribution by Store Type

Boxplot comparisons revealed similar sales volume distributions across online, offline, and hybrid store types, suggesting channel type alone does not strongly differentiate sales performance.
2. Discount Rate vs. Sales Volume

Scatter plot analysis showed a positive trend — higher discount rates tend to correlate with higher sales volume, indicating that promotional pricing has a meaningful impact on driving purchases.
3. Promotion Duration vs. Sales Volume

A slight positive trend was observed between promotion duration and sales volume, however the data showed high variance with wide scatter around the trend line, suggesting promotion duration alone is not a strong predictor of sales.
4. Initial Product Price vs. Discount Rate

No clear relationship was found between initial product price and discount rate — the scatter plot showed a flat trend with highly dispersed data points, indicating discounting strategy appears independent of the original product price.
💡 Insight
Discount rate appears to be the strongest driver of sales volume among the variables analyzed, while initial product pricing shows little to no influence on discount behavior.
📁 Dataset
Retail sales dataset containing store type, sales volume, discount rate, promotion duration, and initial product price.
